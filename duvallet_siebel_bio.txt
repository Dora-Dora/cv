Claire is a PhD student in Eric Alm's lab, where she studies the relationship between the human microbiome and health and disease. She uses biological data to predict and understand disease on the individual and community levels. More generally, Claire is interested in applying personalized medicine approaches for public health.

Outside of lab, Claire works on diversity, equity, and inclusion initiatives in her department and at MIT. She cofounded the BE Application Assistance Program, co-wrote the BE Departmental Values Statement, and is helping shape the future of diversity and inclusion in the Graduate Student Council. Claire is also a trained member of the BE REFS, where she uses conflict coaching skills to advocate for and empower students to improve their graduate experiences. Finally, Claire cofounded the MIT Microbiome Club and MIT-Harvard Microbiome Symposium. In her free time, Claire plays ultimate frisbee with the MIT women's ultimate frisbee team.
